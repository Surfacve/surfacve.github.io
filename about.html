This page is the slower, more personal companion to the journal. It is where I can explain what kind of engineer I am becoming, what holds my attention, and what keeps me returning to software as a craft.

Intro

Who I am, beyond a project list

I am interested in software as both a practical discipline and a way of thinking. I like the mix of structure and curiosity it demands: careful systems, clear writing, thoughtful tradeoffs, and enough experimentation to keep things alive.

I am especially drawn to engineering work that rewards patience. The kind where understanding the shape of a problem matters just as much as writing the code. That includes debugging, architecture, documentation, interface design, and learning tools deeply enough to make them feel calm to use.

"I want the work to be rigorous, but I also want it to stay human."

This site is part notebook, part workshop, and part public record of what I am trying to learn well.

Interests

The themes I keep coming back to

  • Engineering craft I care about readable systems, good naming, reliable behavior, and the small choices that make code easier to maintain.
  • Tools for thinking I am drawn to documentation, note-making, visual explanation, and workflows that help ideas stick instead of disappear.
  • Creative technical work I enjoy the overlap between software, design, writing, and building things in public.
  • Life outside the IDE I also want this page to leave room for the interests, habits, and curiosities that shape how I work.

Right Now

What I am learning and building at the moment

Right now I am using this site as a low-friction place to document what I learn. That includes small web experiments, ideas from talks or books, and the kinds of technical rabbit holes that are too valuable to lose in private notes.

I am also shaping this into a more complete home on the web: one page for ongoing writing, one page for the story behind the work, and one page that distills the strongest engineering achievements into something closer to a public CV.
